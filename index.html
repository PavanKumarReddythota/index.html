In physics and chemistry, matter is defined as any substance that has mass and takes up space by having volume. Key Characteristics of Matter States of Matter: It primarily exists in four states: solid, liquid, gas, and plasma. Transformation: Matter can change from one state to another (e.g., ice melting into water) through heating or cooling. Conservation: In most chemical reactions, matter is neither created nor destroyed; it is merely transformed. Non-Matter Entities Not everything in the universe is matter. Energy phenomena such as light (photons), heat, and sound waves are considered non-matter because they lack rest mass. Similarly, abstract concepts like emotions or ideas do not consist of matter.
